Hello and warm welcome!

I am Olesia, a PhD candidate in cognitive science of language.

My research interests lay mostly in the topics of psycholinguistics and speech processing, as well as embodied cognition.

I currently work on researching speech percpetion in dyslexia with EEG and behavioural methods at Institut Pasteur in Paris.

I am also passionate about the idea of expanding formal research methods with artistic explorations and interested in projects merging science and art.

I always seek for opportunities and collaborations within or around my topics of interest, so please feel free to reach out - let's connect!

Experience

PhD program in Cognitive Science

Junior Researcher

Oct 2022 - Present (Institut Pasteur, Hearing Institute, Paris, France)

I prepare my doctoral thesis in the NeuroSpeech team at the Hearing Institute - Pasteur Institute, under the supervision of Dr. Sophie Bouton and Dr. Anne-Lise Giraud.

As a part of my main PhD project, our team digs into the understanding of cortical oscillations in a dyslexic vs. typical brain using EEG and behavioral tests.
On a practical front, we are searching for solutions that would improve reading in dyslexic children. Particularly, we test remediation programs based on speech rhythmic stimulation and grapho-phonological correspondences.
As a side project, I also experiment with intelligibility of distorted speech.

PLATONOVA O., Goldina S., Neau C., Henke L., Veggiotti L., Masson R., Faye É., Leloup G., Marchesotti S., Giraud A.-L., Bouton S. Comparaison des Thérapies Rythmiques et Grapho-Phonologiques pour la Dyslexie : Résultats Préliminaires. 2024. Rééducation Orthophonique, n°298.
